## §13.1 Capillary Wave Decay

**Config:** `exp13_01_capillary_wave.yaml`
**Setup:** Perturbed circle (l=2, ε=0.05, R₀=0.25), ρ_l=10, ρ_g=1, μ=0.05, σ=1.0, N=128

**Results:**
- Simulation ran 15505 steps to T=10.0 (stable)
- KE shows clear oscillation + decay pattern → capillary physics qualitatively correct
- Volume conservation: |ΔV|/V₀ = 1.89% at T=10

**Issue — D(t) measurement:**
- `_deformation()` (second-moment method) produces noisy signal with ~1100 spurious peaks
- Measured "period" ~0.02 vs theoretical T₀=0.92 — **off by 46×**
- Root cause: the second-moment method measures grid-level fluctuations, not the l=2 modal amplitude
- **Fix needed:** Fourier projection A_l(t) = ∫(ψ − ψ₀) cos(lθ) dA, which directly measures the l=2 mode

**Theoretical reference:**
- ω₀² = l(l−1)(l+2)σ / [(ρ_l+ρ_g)R₀³] → ω₀ = 6.82, T₀ = 0.92
- β = (2l²+4l−1)μ / [(ρ_l+ρ_g)R₀²] = 15 × 0.05 / (11 × 0.0625) = 1.09 (viscous decay)

**Assessment:** Solver captures capillary physics (KE oscillation+decay). Diagnostic metric needs replacement for quantitative validation.

## §13.2 Single Rising Bubble

**Config:** `exp13_02_rising_bubble.yaml`
**Setup:** Gas bubble R=0.25 at (0.5,0.5), ρ_l=10, ρ_g=1, Re=35, Eo=10, 64×128, T=3.0

**Derived physics:** σ = 0.225, μ = 0.101

**Results:**
- 4978 steps to T=3.0 (stable)
- Terminal rise velocity: v_c = 0.160 (mean of last 500 steps)
- Peak velocity: v_c,max = 0.163 at t ≈ 2.0
- Final centroid: y_c = 0.672 (rose 0.172 from initial 0.5)
- **Volume conservation: |ΔV|/V₀ = 2.87 × 10⁻⁵ (excellent)**

**Assessment:** PASS. Bubble rises, reaches terminal velocity, volume is conserved to 0.003%. Qualitatively consistent with Hysing Case 1 behavior (modified ρ ratio). Quantitative comparison with Hysing reference data (ρ=1000) is not meaningful at ρ=10.

## §13.3 Taylor Droplet Deformation

**Config:** `exp13_03_taylor_deformation.yaml`
**Setup:** Neutral buoyancy (ρ_l=ρ_g=1), Couette BC (γ̇=2), R=0.25, N=128, T=5.0

**Sweep:** Ca ∈ {0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4} × λ ∈ {1, 5} (8 cases)

**Results — ALL BLOWUP:**

| Case | t_final | steps | D_ss | D_theory | Status |
|------|---------|-------|------|----------|--------|
| λ=1 Ca=0.1 | 0.006 | 700 | 0.007 | 0.109 | BLOWUP |
| λ=1 Ca=0.2 | 0.006 | 623 | 0.007 | 0.219 | BLOWUP |
| λ=1 Ca=0.3 | 0.007 | 796 | 0.011 | 0.328 | BLOWUP |
| λ=1 Ca=0.4 | 0.007 | 756 | 0.010 | 0.438 | BLOWUP |
| λ=5 Ca=0.1 | 0.015 | 1264 | 0.175 | 0.116 | BLOWUP |
| λ=5 Ca=0.2 | 0.014 | 1249 | 0.234 | 0.231 | BLOWUP |
| λ=5 Ca=0.3 | 0.015 | 1330 | 0.190 | 0.347 | BLOWUP |
| λ=5 Ca=0.4 | 0.014 | 1259 | 0.207 | 0.463 | BLOWUP |

**Root cause analysis:**
1. **CFL too aggressive:** dt ~ 1.4×10⁻⁴ (λ=1) and 3×10⁻⁵ (λ=5) at N=128 with γ̇=2.0 and cfl=0.10. The Couette BC imposes u = ±1.0 at boundaries, giving u_max = 1.0 from step 1.
2. **Explicit viscous term instability:** At N=128, h=0.0078. Viscous CFL: dt < h²/(4ν) = 0.0078²/(4×0.1) = 1.5×10⁻⁴. This is marginal at cfl=0.10.
3. **λ=5 cases survive slightly longer** because μ_l=0.5 provides more dissipation inside the droplet, but the gas-phase viscosity μ_g=0.1 is insufficient to stabilize the outer flow.

**Possible fixes:**
- Reduce grid to N=64 (dt × 4 headroom)
- Reduce γ̇ to 1.0 (halves u_max)
- Use implicit viscous (Crank-Nicolson) — requires CN implementation in ns_pipeline
- Reduce cfl to 0.05

**Assessment:** FAIL. The explicit predictor-corrector is CFL-limited for Couette flows at N=128. This is a known limitation of the explicit viscous scheme documented in §12.3e.

## Cross-Cutting Observations

1. **Volume conservation** is excellent for rising bubble (10⁻⁵) but degraded for capillary wave (10⁻²). The difference is likely due to the capillary wave running 3× longer and the perturbed circle IC being harder to reinitialize.

2. **Explicit viscous scheme** is the bottleneck for Taylor deformation. The Crank-Nicolson scheme validated in exp11_25 would resolve this but is not yet integrated into `TwoPhaseNSSolver`.

3. **Deformation diagnostic** (`_deformation()` via second moments) is inadequate for capillary wave analysis. A Fourier modal decomposition is needed for quantitative validation against Prosperetti (1981).
