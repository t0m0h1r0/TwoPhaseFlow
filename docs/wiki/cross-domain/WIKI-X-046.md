# Paper Success Narrative and Wiki Trial Ledger Have Different Filters

## Claim

The paper and wiki should not contain the same story.  The paper records
contracts that improved the method; the wiki records both those contracts and
the negative paths that narrowed the search.

## Paper Filter

Include a result only when it gives an equation-to-discretization or
equation-to-observable contract: projection-native transport, affine
pressure-history faces, Hodge/phase pressure representatives, volume-weighted
diagnostics, ALE endpoint locality, and residual-converged PPE solves.  GPU
activation, remote execution hygiene, and YAML route flags are reproducibility
or implementation policy, not paper claims.

Place the material by chapter role:

- Chapters 4--11: theory and design formulation.
- Chapters 12--13: verification gates and error-budget acceptance.
- Chapter 14: integrated benchmarks that assume the preceding gates.

## Wiki Filter

Retain failed or inconclusive probes when they prevent repeated detours:
static-grid controls, alternative face/density/phase variants, raw pressure plot
misreadings, GPU route mistakes, YAML audit rules, and rejected tuning knobs.

## Implication

If a future note says "do not put meaningless failures in the paper", it does
not mean "delete failures from the project memory."  It means move them to wiki
with a bounded interpretation.
