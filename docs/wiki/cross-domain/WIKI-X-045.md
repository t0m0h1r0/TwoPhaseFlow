# Rejected Shortcuts Preserve the Theory Boundary

## Claim

Failed shortcuts are useful when they define what must not be mistaken for a
mathematical solution.

## Retained Negative Knowledge

- Damping, CFL reduction, smoothing, curvature caps, and hyperviscosity do not
  prove surface-energy/pressure-work closure.
- GPU caching that changes physics objects or hides backend boundaries is not a
  valid optimization; algebraic temporaries must preserve the discrete contract.
- Raw pressure plot cleanup is not a substitute for a Hodge/phase representative
  tied to the face cochain.
- Long brute-force runs are not a substitute for short analytical and numerical
  gates.

## Effective Use

These rejected paths belong in the wiki, not in the success narrative of the
paper.  They prevent repeated detours while keeping the paper focused on
contracts that improved the solver.

## Implication

Before proposing a fix, ask whether it closes a stated equation-to-discretization
identity.  If not, keep it as negative evidence rather than production theory.
